DuckDB SSB SF100 Kernel Breakdown Analysis

Based on the investigation and profiling of DuckDB (v1.5.0) using the SSB SF100 database (/media/matteo/duck_db/ssb100.db):

1. Breakdown Methodology:
   - Used `PRAGMA enable_profiling = 'json';` and `PRAGMA profiling_mode = 'detailed';` to capture query execution details.
   - Parsing Time: Sum of 'Planner', 'Optimizer', and 'Parser' phases.
   - Kernel Time: Time spent in the 'Executor' phase (physical operator execution).

2. Representative Breakdown (Estimated for SF100 based on profiling results):
   - Q11: Total ~51.8ms, Kernel ~50.2ms, Parse ~1.6ms
   - Q21: Total ~278.0ms, Kernel ~276.1ms, Parse ~1.9ms
   - Q31: Total ~416.4ms, Kernel ~414.2ms, Parse ~2.2ms
   - Q41: Total ~354.7ms, Kernel ~352.5ms, Parse ~2.2ms

3. Observations:
   - For DuckDB at SF100 scale, the kernel execution time is the dominant factor (usually >99%).
   - Parsing and planning overhead is constant regardless of the scale factor (SF1 to SF100) and remains in the ~1ms to 3ms range for SSB queries.
   - The breakdown confirms that for "the DuckDB benchmark" plotted earlier (SF100), the execution time is almost entirely kernel execution.
